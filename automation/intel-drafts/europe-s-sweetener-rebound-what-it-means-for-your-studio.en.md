## The Dip Everyone Noticed — And the Rebound Nobody Planned For

For a few years, the data seemed to confirm what every wellness brand was selling: Europeans were consuming fewer sweeteners. Volume was sliding. Per capita intake was dropping. The narrative wrote itself. Then 2022 arrived, marked the bottom, and the line turned upward.

Total EU-27 sweetener consumption troughed at 2.43 billion kg in 2022 and has been climbing since. Per capita intake followed the same arc — down to 5.47 kg in 2022, then back up, with projections pointing to 6.2 kg by 2028. That is not a rounding error. That is a structural reversal that runs directly counter to the story most fitness and wellness operators have been telling their clients.

## What the Reversal Actually Signals

This is not a story about Europeans abandoning health ambitions. It is a story about complexity. The brief dip through 2019–2022 may reflect genuine behavioural change among health-conscious segments — the very people most likely to join a fitness studio. But the broader population, which sets the aggregate numbers, is now consuming more sweeteners again, and the trend line through 2028 is unambiguous.

For operators, the practical implication is this: your clients do not live in a health bubble. They shop in the same supermarkets, drink in the same cafés, and navigate the same food environment as everyone else. If per capita sweetener intake in their world is rising, assuming they are successfully reducing their own is a coaching blind spot.

The per capita uptick was already visible in 2023 at 5.55 kg — before most studio owners had updated a single piece of nutrition content. By 2024 it reached 5.68 kg. The gap between what operators are messaging and what clients are actually experiencing is growing.

## The Operator Move

The studios that win the next three years will not be the ones shouting 'cut sugar.' They will be the ones who acknowledge the environment their clients actually inhabit and build programming around it.

That means nutrition coaching framed around navigating sweetener exposure rather than eliminating it. It means onboarding conversations that ask about dietary context without shame. It means metabolic literacy content that lands as practical, not preachy — because by the time the market reaches the projected trajectory toward 2.75 billion kg of total EU volume in 2028, 'just avoid it' will be an increasingly hollow instruction.

The data has shifted. The question is whether your programming has.
