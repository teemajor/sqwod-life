# LLM Council — Blog Illustration Style (2026-06-30)

## Original question
"I can't decide which illustration style to use for all 45 blog cover images. Help."

## Framed question
Which single illustration style to lock for all 45 blog article cover images (and every future post) on sqwod.life / sqwodpod.com — the top-of-funnel "Pod Guides" blog for Sqwod Pod, a Berlin private-gym brand. Brand: strict high-contrast BLACK-AND-WHITE, minimal, premium, rebel, Berlin. Blog's job: reach on social + funnel to bookings. Audience: coaches/PTs/gym founders + busy professionals, DE/EN. Production: AI-generated via Imagen 4 (~$2/45, reproducible, human QC, ~1-in-5 misfire). Candidates: (1) Minimalist single-line art B&W; (2) Manhwa/webtoon comic B&W; (3) Sports poster mixed-media (colorful → would lock to B&W + one accent); (4) PJ Kaiowa heavy-ink graphic-novel B&W.

## Anonymization map (revealed)
A = Expansionist · B = Outsider · C = Contrarian · D = First Principles · E = Executor

## Advisor responses
**Contrarian → Line Art (#1):** Two contradictory jobs, one variable; the loud Poster is a seductive trap. Blog's job is funnel-to-bookings, not winning the feed. AI consistency across 45 is the unspoken killer — "1 in 5 misfire" is fantasy for manhwa/ink; faces, anatomy, line weight drift into AI slop. Line art is the only style Imagen keeps reliably on-model. Test thumbnail legibility + longer German headlines first.

**First Principles → Heavy Ink (#4):** B&W in a color feed IS the pattern interrupt — "B&W vs scroll-stopping" is a false tension, which kills #3. Real question: calm vs dramatic. Brand = rebel/premium/high-contrast, so calm line art contradicts three brand words; heavy ink literally embodies "high-contrast." Biggest reframe: lock a recurring COMPOSITION TEMPLATE (crop, negative-space headline zone, focal weight) — consistency lives in layout, not rendering.

**Expansionist → Sports Poster (#3):** You're choosing a 3-year signature visual language. The others are commodity-adjacent and vanish. Poster + ONE accent = a proprietary asset (your "Tiffany blue"), recognized before the name, most merch/screen-print-ready (posters, signage, apparel), accent color as a strategic dial. Loud wins reach for a rebel Berlin brand.

**Outsider → Manhwa (#2):** Reacting cold: line art reads "empty/yoga," scroll past. Manhwa shows a person/expression/story — the only option where I register WHO it's for; on-brand, premium, not loud. Sports poster clashes with strict B&W, reads cheap-stock/supplement, generic cliché. Heavy ink tips dark/grim — horror, not wellness. Winner: Manhwa, the only one with a human face + story hook.

**Executor → Manhwa (#2):** Real cost is the QC/regen loop at weekly cadence. Line art is the trap: single-line is hardest for Imagen, high SILENT-misfire that drifts the blog. Heavy ink = max hands/faces = ~1-in-3 failure. Poster = fight color-lock + halftone chaos = highest garble. Manhwa = model's comfort zone (most-trained style), lowest misfire, tolerates abstract topics via metaphor, premium B&W without photoreal hands. One master prompt, swap the subject line, 2-minute weekly covers.

## Chairman synthesis

**Where the council agrees**
- Drop the **Sports Poster (#3)**. Three advisors + every reviewer flag it: its power comes from color, so forcing it to strict B&W ships a compromised version of the weakest-fit candidate. B&W in a color-saturated feed is *already* the scroll-stop.
- The real axis is **calm vs dramatic**, and the brand (rebel, premium, high-contrast) leans dramatic — which eliminates the "calm" framing advantage of line art.
- **Production reliability is decisive** at 45 + weekly. The fragile styles (single-line, heavy-ink) fail silently or on hands/faces; manhwa is the model's comfort zone.

**Where the council clashes**
- *Line art* (Contrarian: most on-model/safe) vs *Executor* (line art is the trap — silent drift, reads "empty"). Reviewers sided with the Executor: line art's "safety" is an untested assumption and it under-reads at thumbnail size.
- *Heavy ink #4* (First Principles: embodies "high-contrast," most premium) vs *reliability + tone* (Executor: worst misfire rate; Outsider: too grim for wellness topics).
- *Manhwa* sits in the productive middle: dramatic + on-brand + reliable + has a human face/story hook.

**Blind spots the council caught**
- No one proposed **measuring** — though in practice you've already done a visual bake-off across all four. The eyeball test is essentially complete.
- The **headline overlay is the real job** of a cover image. German runs ~30% longer than English — so whatever style wins, it must reserve a clean negative-space zone for a bilingual headline. (First Principles' "lock the template" is the sharpest practical insight.)
- This is **not** a permanent 3-year lock — regenerating later is cheap. Decide, ship, revisit.

**The recommendation**
Lock **Manhwa / webtoon comic in black & white (#2)** — but adopt the First Principles discipline: lock a **composition template**, not just a look. Fixed crop, subject weighted to one side, a consistent clean negative-space zone for the DE/EN headline, and a consistent heavy-black ratio. This gives the dramatic, premium, on-brand, scroll-stopping image with the **lowest production risk** and the system-level consistency that survives 45 covers and weekly output. Keep the heavy-ink (#4) intensity in your back pocket for occasional flagship/hero pieces. Drop Sports Poster and Line Art.

**The one thing to do first**
Before batching all 45: lock the **composition template** by generating 3 manhwa covers built to one exact crop + negative-space headline zone (one action, one portrait, one abstract topic like "pricing your services"), then drop a long German headline on each to confirm legibility. If those 3 read as one system with the headline on, run the batch.
