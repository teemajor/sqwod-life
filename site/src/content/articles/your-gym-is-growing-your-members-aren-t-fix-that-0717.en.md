## The industry is booming. So why does something feel off?

The DSSV just dropped the key figures for the DACH fitness industry in 2026, and the headline is good: the sector is growing. More clubs, more members, more revenue moving through the system. Operators are opening champagne.

But here's the quiet problem nobody wants to say out loud: **growth in memberships is not the same as growth in fitness outcomes.** And in a market where consumers are getting sharper about what they're actually buying, that gap is going to hurt.

The industry is selling access. Members want transformation. That's not a marketing problem — it's a product problem.

## What Lars Springer actually proves

Trainingsworld just featured Lars Springer, who became European Champion. Read past the trophy and there's a useful signal buried in his story: elite results come from structured, intentional programming — not just showing up and sweating.

Springer's journey is a case study in what disciplined, goal-oriented training looks like when it actually works. And here's the uncomfortable mirror that holds up to the average gym floor: most members have no structure, no clear goal ladder, and no one checking whether they're progressing.

The DACH data tells you the market is big. Lars Springer tells you what the best version of a fitness product looks like. The distance between those two things is your opportunity — or your competitor's.

## The operator's real competitive moat in 2026

Every gym in the DACH region is competing on location, price, and equipment. That race has a floor, and most operators are already on it. The clubs that will pull away are the ones that compete on **coached outcomes** — not just facility access.

What does that mean practically?

- **Programme your floor, not just your timetable.** Members should be able to see a 12-week path, not just today's class. Springer didn't get to a European title by winging it session by session.
- **Make progress visible.** If a member can't articulate what they're training *for* this month, they're a churn risk. Build in check-ins, benchmarks, something that makes progress feel real.
- **Use champion stories internally.** The Springer profile isn't just content for enthusiasts — it's a coaching tool. Show your members what intentional training actually looks like. Aspiration is a retention mechanism.

The DSSV's figures confirm the market is there. The question is whether you're building something worth staying in.

## The brief

The DACH fitness market is growing on volume. The operators who win the next cycle won't be the ones who signed the most members — they'll be the ones who made members feel like they were actually getting somewhere. Structure beats access. Outcomes beat amenities. And if a European champion's training arc can teach a gym-goer in Munich or Zurich what real progress looks like, that's not just inspiration — that's a retention strategy with legs.
