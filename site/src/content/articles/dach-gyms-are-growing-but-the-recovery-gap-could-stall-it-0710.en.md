## The Good News Has a Hidden Problem

The DACH fitness market is growing. The DSSV just dropped its 2026 industry figures, and the headline is hard to argue with: membership numbers are up, studios are expanding, and the business of getting people moving is genuinely healthy.

But here's the trap inside every growth story — when the market expands fast, the operators who survive the next contraction are the ones who built something stickier than a treadmill and a locker room. Right now, most DACH gyms are competing on floor space and price. That's a race to the bottom with a very predictable finish line.

The question isn't whether the market is growing. It's whether *your* facility is growing the right things.

## What Members Actually Want Next

The Global Wellness Institute has been tracking a quiet but accelerating shift: contrast hydrotherapy — the deliberate alternation between hot and cold water exposure — is moving from elite athlete recovery rooms into mainstream wellness facilities. Cold plunge, hot pool, repeat. It sounds simple. The demand signal is not.

Wellness-oriented members aren't just looking for a workout. They're looking for a full recovery loop. They want to train hard *and* recover smart, ideally in the same building, ideally on the same visit. When a gym can close that loop, the member's reason to leave shrinks dramatically. Churn drops. Spend per visit climbs. The facility stops being a commodity.

The DSSV data confirms the macro tailwind — people across Germany, Austria, and Switzerland are investing in their physical health at scale. But investment in health isn't the same as loyalty to a specific club. Loyalty comes from irreplaceability. A solid squat rack does not make you irreplaceable. A contrast hydrotherapy circuit that leaves someone feeling genuinely restored? That's harder to walk away from.

## The Operator Move Right Now

You don't need to gut-renovate your facility to test this. Here's the practical path:

**Audit your recovery real estate.** Most mid-size DACH clubs have underutilized wet areas, sauna rooms running at half capacity, or unused square footage near changing rooms. That's your starting point, not a blank canvas build.

**Start with the protocol, not just the hardware.** Contrast hydrotherapy is as much about the structured experience — the sequence, the timing, the guidance — as it is about the equipment. A cold plunge next to a hot tub with no instruction is just plumbing. A coached recovery session with a defined hot-cold protocol is a product you can price, market, and upsell.

**Position it as a premium tier, not a perk.** The DSSV's growth figures suggest DACH consumers are willing to spend on fitness. That willingness doesn't stop at the gym floor. Recovery memberships, add-on sessions, and small-group recovery classes are real revenue lines — not amenities you apologize for charging for.

The DACH market is in a growth window. Windows close. The operators who use this moment to build genuine differentiation — not just more machines — are the ones who will still be talking about growth when the next industry report drops.

Everyone else will be talking about discounts.
