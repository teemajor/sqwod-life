## Everyone's Opening. Almost Nobody's Standing Out.

The DACH fitness market is growing. The DSSV made that official with their freshly released *Eckdaten der Fitnesswirtschaft DACH 2026* — the sector is expanding, studios are multiplying, and consumer demand for structured training isn't cooling off. Great news, right?

Sure. Until you realize that a rising tide also lifts every competitor on your street. When the market grows and every operator benefits equally, growth stops being your moat. It becomes the floor. And if every studio in your city is posting the same reformer Pilates reels and the same protein-shake partnerships, you have a volume problem disguised as a marketing problem.

The real question isn't *are people joining gyms.* It's *why would they join yours.*

## The Answer Is Probably a Person

Here's the signal buried in the noise: Trainingsworld recently spotlighted Lars Springer, who claimed the European Champion title in his discipline. Read the piece and what jumps out isn't the podium — it's the story. The training philosophy, the sacrifices, the specific methods. The *person* behind the result.

That's not an accident. Human narratives convert. They give a prospect someone to root for, something to believe in, a reason to walk through *your* door instead of the one down the road.

Most fitness operators are sitting on exactly this kind of asset and doing nothing with it. Your head coach who competed regionally. Your member who dropped 40kg and now coaches others. Your founder who built the programming from scratch. These are not background details. They are your differentiation strategy — if you're willing to treat them that way.

The DSSV data tells us the market wants fitness. Lars Springer's story tells us what the market actually responds to. Connect those two dots and you have a playbook.

## Do This Before Your Competitor Does

You don't need a European champion on staff. You need a *character* — a real person with a real arc that your target member can see themselves in or be inspired by.

Here's the move, right now:

**1. Audit your human capital.** Walk your floor this week and list every coach, instructor, or long-term member who has a result, a credential, a comeback, or a competition history worth talking about. Write it down. You probably have more than you think.

**2. Build one story, not a campaign.** Pick the most compelling person. Sit down with them for 20 minutes. Record it on your phone. Ask three questions: What did you used to struggle with? What changed? What do you believe now that you didn't before? That's your content for the next month.

**3. Make it structural, not seasonal.** The DSSV's *DACH-Studie 2026* confirms this market is continuing to grow — meaning competition will keep intensifying. One hero story isn't enough long-term. Build a rotating feature into your content calendar. New story every quarter. Coach spotlight. Member milestone. Competition recap.

The operators who treat their people as product will compound. The ones still leading with equipment specs and square footage will get buried in the noise of a booming, increasingly crowded market.

Growth is the context. People are the strategy. Start there.
