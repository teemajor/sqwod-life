## The Numbers Are Fine. The Story Is Missing.

The DSSV just released the key figures for the DACH fitness industry 2026, and the headline is broadly stable: the sector is holding its ground. Memberships, facilities, revenue — the structural foundation looks solid. Operators should feel reassured, right?

Not quite. Stable isn't the same as growing. And in a market where every gym chain, boutique studio, and app is fighting for the same recurring monthly fee, "holding ground" is just a slow retreat dressed up in decent clothes. The operators pulling ahead aren't the ones with the best equipment-to-squat-rack ratio. They're the ones with the best story.

## Lars Springer Just Handed You a Blueprint

This week, Trainingsworld covered Lars Springer's European Championship win — a story that, on the surface, looks like sports coverage. Read it again. It's a masterclass in what actually moves people off the couch and through your door.

Springer isn't famous. He's not a social media giant with a nine-figure following. He's a competitive athlete who trained, competed, and won at the European level — the kind of person who exists inside your gym right now, completely invisible in your marketing.

That's the gap. The DSSV data tells us the DACH fitness market has scale. Springer's story tells us what gives that scale meaning. People don't join gyms for treadmills. They join because they see someone like them achieve something they didn't think was possible. That's the emotional trigger. That's what converts a browser into a member and a member into an evangelist.

Fitness operators are sitting on a gold mine of these stories and doing absolutely nothing with them.

## Your Do-This-Now Move

Stop waiting for a brand ambassador deal you can't afford. Start a "member spotlight" content engine this week — not a polished ad campaign, a genuine story series. Here's the framework:

**Find your Lars.** Every gym has one. The member who just competed in their first powerlifting meet, finished a triathlon, lost a significant amount of weight, or hit a personal milestone they'd given up on. Ask your front desk team. They already know who these people are.

**Tell the real story.** Not a before-and-after photo with a caption. A proper short narrative — what they were struggling with, what the training looked like, what the win felt like. Trainingsworld ran Springer's story because it was *specific*. Specificity is what makes people feel seen.

**Distribute it where your prospects already are.** Your gym's Instagram, your email newsletter, a printed card in the changing room. The channel matters less than the consistency. One story a month, done well, beats twelve forgettable posts a week.

The DACH market data from DSSV shows the industry has a strong base to build on. But base isn't destiny. In a stable market, differentiation is everything — and differentiation lives in the stories only you can tell.

Lars Springer didn't need a PR agency. He needed someone to pay attention. Your next best marketing campaign is probably already training on your floor. Go find them.
