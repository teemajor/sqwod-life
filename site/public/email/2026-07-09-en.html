Connect the dots
Your members will sell for you — if you let them
There's a reason cult studios outgrow their square footage before they outgrow their waiting lists: belonging is stickier than any promo code. The operators winning right now aren't just programming great classes — they're designing moments that make members feel like insiders, then stepping back while those members do the recruiting.
So what does that look like in practice? Referral loops built around identity, not incentives. A member who says 'this is my community' will bring three friends; a member chasing a free class brings one, maybe. Audit your touchpoints this week — onboarding, milestones, social proof — and ask whether each one deepens belonging or just delivers a service. The ones that deepen it are your real marketing budget.
