Garmin + TrainingPeaks: who really owns your programming?
TrainingPeaks has long been the invisible backbone of serious training — coaches build plans there, athletes live there, and everyone quietly assumed it would stay neutral. Garmin just ended that assumption. An acquisition folds a software platform into a hardware ecosystem, and the history of those mergers is pretty consistent: integrations tighten, pricing evolves, and the new parent's devices get the best data treatment first.
For studio operators and coaches, the concrete risk is dependency. If your entire client programming stack lives in one platform that now answers to a device manufacturer, your leverage just shrank. This week's action is simple: audit where your programming actually lives. Do you have exportable copies? Are your athletes locked to a Garmin-native experience to get full value? Diversification isn't paranoia — it's ops hygiene.
