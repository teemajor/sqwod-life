The fitness business rewards the second look
An inconclusive autopsy, a property tax bill that quietly compounded, a wearable nobody wore past week two — the theme isn't bad luck, it's deferred attention. Operators who only check the obvious numbers (revenue, retention) keep getting surprised by the stuff living one layer down: how their square footage is assessed, whether the gear they recommend actually sticks, what slips through the cracks during a packed coaching day.
The flip side is also true. Fitbod's CEO has been watching the same signal since day one — resistance training doesn't fade — and that patience is now being validated by where AI coaching tools are placing their bets. The operators winning right now aren't the ones reacting to trends; they're the ones who looked closer, earlier, and kept looking.
