Connect the dots
Big tech wants your niche. Your ops will save or sink you.
Every story this week is really the same story: the gap between what a brand promises and what it actually delivers at the point of contact. Peloton buying Skōp, Garmin dropping a capable mid-tier watch, Fitbod doubling down on strength — these are all platforms saying 'we can own your customer's whole fitness life.' The threat is real, but it's abstract.
The concrete threat is operational. The member who showed up to cancel and found nobody home? That's your reputation bleeding out through a billing vendor. The coach who loses the breakthrough insight because their hands are full? That's a retention moment dropped on the floor. Big platforms win on distribution; independent operators win on execution. This week's news is a checklist, not a eulogy — if your cancellation flow, your session notes, and your programming anchor are tight, the platforms are competing for a customer you've already locked in.

Peloton acquires connected Pilates brand Skōp
Peloton just bought Skōp, a connected Pilates innovator, signaling the struggling hardware giant is betting on portfolio breadth to stay relevant — if you run a Pilates-adjacent studio, the big platforms are officially coming for your lane.
