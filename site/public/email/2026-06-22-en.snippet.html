Connect the dots
The fitness industry is eating its own homework
Peloton buying a connected Pilates brand, Garmin dropping a $349 runner's watch, and Fitbod's CEO reminding everyone that strength training never left — these aren't separate headlines. They're the same story: hardware and software companies are racing to own the full-stack fitness experience before studios do it themselves. The discipline doesn't matter. The data does.
For operators, the pattern is clear: every category you're not capturing digitally is a category someone with a balance sheet is circling. Connected Pilates just got acquired. Connected strength is already commoditized. The question isn't whether your modality will get the Peloton treatment — it's whether you'll still be relevant when it does.
